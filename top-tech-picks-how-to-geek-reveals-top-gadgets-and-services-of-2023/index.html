Top Tech Picks: How-To Geek Reveals Top Gadgets and Services of 2023

It’s easy to debate the best or worst products of the year, but these are How-To Geek’s 12 favorites from 2023. This isn’t just a hypothetical list of products that look cool or seem like they might be good. Our reviews team went hands-on with over 350 tech and lifestyle products this year and narrowed it down to 12—with a few extra honor mentions thrown in for good measure, too. From laptops to speakers to smart glasses, there was a lot to like in 2023.

Lenovo Legion 9i Gen 8

Sergio Rodriguez / How-To Geek

The Lenovo Legion 9i Gen 8 is a powerful laptop aimed at playing top-tier games and has a lot of features to support that goal. There’s a capable CPU and GPU, as well as per-key RGB lights and an integrated liquid cooling system, to name a few.

Why it’s great: As reviewer Cianna Garrison writes, “Serious gamers want serious performance from a gaming laptop, and for the most part, the 16-inch Lenovo Legion 9i (2023) serves up gaming on a silver platter. With a top-tier NVIDIA GPU and an HX-rated Intel Core i9 processor, the Legion 9i offers speed, accuracy, a gorgeous chassis, and a 3.2K display for all the eye candy you could want.”

Google Pixel 8 Pro

Justin Duino / How-To Geek

Google’s Pixel 8 Pro brings all of the company’s cool new photo and AI tech to consumers in the form of a big, beautiful smartphone. It also does this with almost no compromises or concerning flaws—a departure from past Pixel phones.

Why it’s great: The most powerful Pixel phone gets a camera with impressive zoom as well as the ability to reposition people’s faces to get the Best Take. If you want Google’s latest AI advancements in the keyboard and sprinkled throughout Android, then this is the phone to get.

The Ugreen Nexode 100W 2-in-1 Gan Charging Station is a versatile power hub for all kinds of devices, including MagSafe-compatible iPhones. The Nexode 100W is 2.35 inches wide and 3.5 inches tall, making it suitable for use on a desk or small enough to pack and bring on a trip.

Why it’s great: There are lots of great charging devices, but this Ugreen accessory fills a wide range of needs with enough power for plenty of consumer laptop models or anything else that doesn’t need a data connection. The tilting iPhone charger allows StandBy mode to shine.

Nomad 65W Slim Power Adapter

On paper, very little is exciting about the Nomad 65W Slim Power Adapter . It’s a charger that features dual USB-C ports and is capable of producing up to 65W of power. Its winning characteristic is its small size, thanks to GaN technology.

Why it’s great: The true value of the 65W Slim adapter is that it’s slim and small enough to fit in a front jeans pocket. This GaN device could significantly cut down on the weight and bulk of the items you travel with but is still powerful enough to charge a MacBook Air or Surface laptop.

Sonos Era 300

Tyler Hayes / How-To Geek

The Sonos Era 300 is one of the first consumer speakers to go all-in on spatial audio, built for Dolby Atmos music. It features an up-firing driver and plenty of oomph to fill a living room or act as a rear surround sound speaker when two Era 300s are paired with a Sonos soundbar.

Why it’s great: Dolby Atmos mixed tracks feature depth, height, and wide-reaching instrumentation. The Era 300 is truly a transformational speaker for listening at home. It’s compact but able to produce rich and well-rounded music. It has a line-in, Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, and AirPlay connectivity. Plus, when used as surround sound speakers, it gives movies real height and dimension.

DJI Mini 4 Pro

Bill Loguidice / How-To Geek

The DJI Mini 4 Pro is a slick, prosumer drone that packs a lot of previously high-end features into a more affordable package for people who want cinematic shots.

Why it’s great: The Mini 4 Pro might not be an entry-level drone because of its price, but it’s dead simple to use for beginners or enthusiasts. There are lots of automated and pre-set shot types that do all the work and look spectacular captured on the integrated camera.

DJI Mini 4 Pro

The DJI Mini 4 Pro does just about everything you would want out of a drone in an incredibly compact, convenient, and highly functional package. The ease-of-use and image quality are best in class.

Hannah Stryker / How-To Geek

The Amazon Echo Dot with Clock (5th-Gen) keeps the same sphere form factor as the previous model but adds in some new features like a temperature sensor to get local temps and an accelerometer for gesture controls.

Why it’s great: Having a built-in temperature sensor means you can ask Alexa about how cold it is in your room. The accelerometer allows you to hit or tap the Dot to snooze an alarm or to stop timers.

Amazon Echo Dot with Clock (5th Gen)

While the looks may not have changed, the Amazon Echo Dot with Clock (5th Gen) has new temperature sensors and an accelerometer, as well as a larger driver inside for better sound quality.

The Ember Tumbler is a 16-ounce mug that keeps drinks hot between 120F and 145F (50C-62.5C) for up to three hours. No need to rush to finish your morning coffee or tea.

Honorable Mentions

Sergio Rodriguez / How-To Geek

There were a plethora of neat electronics and products released in 2023 that didn’t make the cut for the list but are still worth highlighting. For example, the Boox Palma is a phone-sized eReader that excels at portability and makes reading on the go easier when it can quickly be stuffed into nearly any pocket.

The iPhone 15 Pro might look similar to last year’s model, but its titanium frame makes it feel lighter and nicer to hold. Plus, Apple’s latest phone continues to get better at photography.

The Rivian R1S is a stunning electric vehicle. It may cost too much for a lot of people to afford, but its 7-seat configuration, comfortable ride, and off-road capabilities make it a truly special SUV.

Ebikes continue to take over the mobility space, and the Tenways CGO600 Pro is a smooth, fun ride that’s also lightweight and easy to maneuver wherever you store it.
